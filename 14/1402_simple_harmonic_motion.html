1402 Simple Harmonic Motion

The force acting on a stretched ideal spring as Fx=kxF_x = kxFx​=kx. The x-component of force the spring exerts on the body is the negative of this, so

Fx=−kx(1)\begin{aligned} F_x &= -kx \quad &\text{(1)} \end{aligned} Fx​​=−kx​(1)​

where FxF_xFx​ is the restoring force exerted by an ideal spring, kkk is the force constant of spring and xxx is the displacement of the spring.
When the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement from equilibrium, as given by Eq. (1), the oscillation is called simple harmonic motion (SHM). The acceleration is

ax=d2xdt2=−kmx(2)\begin{aligned} a_x = \frac{d^2 x}{dt^2} = -\frac{k}{m}x \quad &\text{(2)} \end{aligned} ax​=dt2d2x​=−mk​x​(2)​

Displacement, Velocity, and Acceleration in SHM

x(t)=Acos⁡(ωt+ϕ)(3)\begin{aligned} x (t) &= A\cos(\omega t + \phi) \quad &\text{(3)} \end{aligned} x(t)​=Acos(ωt+ϕ)​(3)​

We find the velocity vxv_xvx​ and acceleration axa_xax​ as functions of time for a harmonic oscillator by taking derivatives of Eq. (3) with respect to time:

vx=dxdt=−Aωsin⁡(ωt+ϕ)ax=dvdt=d2xdt2=−Aω2cos⁡(ωt+ϕ)ax=−ω2x(t)\begin{aligned} v_x &= \frac{dx}{dt} = -A\omega \sin(\omega t + \phi) \\ a_x &= \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{d^2 x}{dt^2} = -A\omega^2 \cos(\omega t + \phi)\\ a_x &= -\omega^2 x(t) \end{aligned} vx​ax​ax​​=dtdx​=−Aωsin(ωt+ϕ)=dtdv​=dt2d2x​=−Aω2cos(ωt+ϕ)=−ω2x(t)​
