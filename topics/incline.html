1. Frictionless incline. Force FFF is applied upon mass m=10kgm=10kgm=10kg.

a). Find FFF, so that mmm moves up along the incline at constant velocity.
b). Find FNF_NFN​ (Normal Force).

Solution

a. Constant velocity indicates zero acceleration.

∑Fx=Fx−Wx=0Fx=Fcos⁡θWx=mgsin⁡θ⇒F=mgsin⁡θcos⁡θ=10∗9.8∗sin⁡30∘cos⁡30∘≈57.7N\begin{aligned} \sum F_x &= F_x - W_x = 0\\ F_x &= F\cos \th\\ W_x &= mg\sin \th\\ \To F &= \frac{mg \sin \th}{\cos\th} = \frac{10*9.8*\sin 30^{\circ}}{\cos 30^{\circ}} \approx 57.7 N \end{aligned} ∑Fx​Fx​Wx​⇒F​=Fx​−Wx​=0=Fcosθ=mgsinθ=cosθmgsinθ​=cos30∘10∗9.8∗sin30∘​≈57.7N​

b. In yyy-direction, the net force is zero too.

∑Fy=FN−Wy−Fy=0Wy=mgcos⁡θFy=Fsin⁡θ⇒FN=mgcos⁡θ+Fsin⁡θ=10∗9.8∗cos⁡30∘+57.7sin⁡30∘≈115.5N\begin{aligned} \sum F_y &= F_N - W_y - F_y = 0\\ W_y &= mg\cos \th\\ F_y &= F\sin\th\\ \To F_N &= mg\cos \th + F\sin \th\\ & = 10*9.8*\cos 30^{\circ} + 57.7 \sin 30^{\circ} \approx 115.5N \end{aligned} ∑Fy​Wy​Fy​⇒FN​​=FN​−Wy​−Fy​=0=mgcosθ=Fsinθ=mgcosθ+Fsinθ=10∗9.8∗cos30∘+57.7sin30∘≈115.5N​
