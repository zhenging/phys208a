The Impulse-Momentum Theorem: The impulse of the net force on a particle during a time interval equals to the change in momentum of that particle during that interval.
It also holds when forces are not constant. To see this, we integrate both sides of Newton's second law ∑F⃗=dp⃗dt\sum \vec F = \frac{d\vec p}{dt}∑F=dtdp​​ over time between t1t_1t1​ and t2t_2t2​
