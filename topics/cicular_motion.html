Circular Motion

Important Idea: Any object undergoing circular motion will experience acceleration (centripetal acceleration a⃗c\vec a_cac​ or a⃗r\vec a_rar​), and by Newton's Second Law, it takes a Net Force to "force" that obect to undergo circular motion. That force is the centripetal force (FcF_cFc​ or FrF_rFr​), which is proportional to centripetal acceleration a⃗c\vec a_cac​.

F⃗c=ma⃗c=mv2R\vec F_c = m\vec a_c = \frac{mv^2}{R} Fc​=mac​=Rmv2​

2. A car is at the bottom of a circular track with radius of 101010m, and moves along the track.
a. Find the initial velocity v0v_0v0​, so that it just makes over the top.

Solution

At the top of the track, the centripetal force is provided solely by the car's weight and the normal force is zero. Let the centripetal force be FcF_cFc​, the weight of the car be www, and the normal be FNF_NFN​.
1. According to Newton's Second Law

Fc=mvf2R=w−FNw=mgFN=0⇒vf=gR\begin{aligned} F_c &= \frac{mv_f^2}{R} = w - F_N\\ w &= mg\\ F_N &= 0\\ \To v_f &= \sqrt {gR} \end{aligned} Fc​wFN​⇒vf​​=Rmvf2​​=w−FN​=mg=0=gR​​

2. By Conservation of Energy
