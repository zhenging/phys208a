1308 Black Hole

Black holes, the Schwarzschild Radius, and the Event Horizon

If a nonrotating spherical mass distribution with total mass M has a radius less than its Schwarzschild radius RSR_SRS​, it is called a black hole. The gravitational interaction prevents anything, including light, from escaping from within a sphere with radius RSR_SRS​.

RS=2GMc2\begin{aligned} R_S = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \end{aligned} RS​=c22GM​​

RSR_SRS​ - Schwarzschild radius of a black hole.
GGG - Gravitational constant.
MMM - Mass of black hole.
ccc - Speed of light in vacuum
