1406 The Physical Pendulum

A Physical Pendulum is any real pendulum that uses an extended body, as contrasted to the idealized simple pendulum with all of its mass concentrated at a point.

Figure 14.23 shows a body of irregular shape pivoted so that it can turn without friction about an axis through point OOO. When the body is displaced as shown, the weight mg causes a restoring torque

τ=−(mg)(dsin⁡θ)(1)\begin{aligned} \tau = -(mg) (d\sin \th) \quad &\text{(1)} \end{aligned} τ=−(mg)(dsinθ)​(1)​

When the body is releases, it oscillates about its equilibrium position. The motion is not SHM becaues the torque τ\tauτ is proportional to sin⁡θ\sin \thsinθ rather than to θ\thθ. However, if θ\thθ is small, we can approximate sin⁡θ\sin \thsinθ by θ\thθ in radians. Then the motion is approximately SHM.

τ=−mgd(θ)(2)\begin{aligned} \tau &= -mgd(\th) \quad &\text{(2)} \end{aligned} τ​=−mgd(θ)​(2)​

The torque and angular acceleration have the relation that ∑τ=Iα\sum \tau = I \alpha∑τ=Iα,

τ=−mgd(θ)=Iα=Id2θdt2d2θdt2=−mgdIθ(3)\begin{gathered} \tau = -mgd(\th) = I \alpha = I \frac{d^2 \th}{d t^2}\\ \frac{d^2 \th}{d t^2} = -\frac{mgd}{I}\th \quad &\text{(3)} \end{gathered} τ=−mgd(θ)=Iα=Idt2d2θ​dt2d2θ​=−Imgd​θ​(3)​

Comparing equation (3) with a=d2xdt2=−kmxa = \dfrac{d^2 x}{dt^2}= -\dfrac{k}{m}xa=dt2d2x​=−mk​x for the spring-mass system, the quantity (mgdI\dfrac{mgd}{I}Imgd​) and km\dfrac{k}{m}mk​ play the same role. Thus the angular frequency is

ω=mgdI(4)\begin{aligned} \omega &= \sqrt{\frac{mgd}{I}} \quad &\text{(4)} \end{aligned} ω​=Imgd​​​(4)​

where mmm is the mass of the object, ddd is the distance from the center of mass to the pivot point and III is the moment of inertia. (can be obtained by parallel axis-theorem)
The period TTT is

T=2πω=2πImgd(5)\begin{aligned} T &= \frac{2\pi}{\omega} = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{I}{mgd}} \quad &\text{(5)} \end{aligned} T​=ω2π​=2πmgdI​​​(5)​
