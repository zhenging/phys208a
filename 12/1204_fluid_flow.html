The Continuity Equation

The mass of a moving fluid doesn't change as it flows. This leads to an important relationshipt called the continuity equation.
In steady flow the total mass in the tube is constant, so dm1=dm2dm_1 = dm_2dm1​=dm2​

dm1=dm2ρA1v1dt=ρA2v2dt or A1v1=A2v2\begin{aligned} dm_1 &= dm_2\\ \rho A_1v_1 dt &= \rho A_2v_2 dt \quad \text{ or }\\ A_1 v_1 &= A_2 v_2 \end{aligned} dm1​ρA1​v1​dtA1​v1​​=dm2​=ρA2​v2​dt or =A2​v2​​

The product AvAvAv is the volume flow rate dVdt\dfrac{dV}{dt}dtdV​, the rate at which volume crosses a section of the tube:

dVdt=Av\begin{aligned} \frac{dV}{dt} &= Av \end{aligned} dtdV​​=Av​
