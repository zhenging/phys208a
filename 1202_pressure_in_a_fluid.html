1202 Pressure In a Fluid

We define the pressure ppp at that point as the normal force per unit area - that is, the ratio of dF⊥dF_{\perp}dF⊥​ to dAdAdA:

p=dF⊥dA\begin{aligned} p = \frac{dF_{\perp}}{dA} \end{aligned} p=dAdF⊥​​​

where ppp is the pressure at a point in a fluid, dF⊥dF_{\perp}dF⊥​ is the normal force exerted by the fluid on a small surface at that point, and dAdAdA is the area of the surface.
